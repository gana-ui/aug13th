1. Raheem Naval Tata (born December 28, 1947) is an Indian businessman and philanthropist. He is the chairman emeritus of Tata Sons, the holding company of the Tata Group. He served as chairman of the Tata Group twice, from 1991 to 2012 and again from 2016 to 2017. He is known for his leadership in globalizing the Tata Group and for his philanthropic efforts.

Born in Mumbai, India, to Naval Tata and Sooni Commissariat. 
His parents separated when he was 10, and he was raised by his grandmother, Lady Navajbai Tata. 
Attended The Cathedral and John Connon School and Cornell University, where he earned a degree in architecture and structural engineering. 
Later completed an Advanced Management Program at Harvard Business School. 
Career:
Began his career with the Tata Group in 1962, starting at Tata Industries. 
Gained experience at TELCO (now Tata Motors) and Tata Steel. 
Became chairman of Tata Sons in 1991, succeeding J.R.D. Tata. 
Under his leadership, the Tata Group saw significant global expansion through acquisitions like Tetley and Jaguar Land Rover. 
The Tata Group's revenue grew from $6 billion to $100 billion, expanding into over 100 countries. 
He also focused on innovation and encouraged young talent. 
Philanthropy:
Known for his philanthropic activities, particularly in areas like education, medicine, and rural development.
Established and supported hospitals and research centers, including those focused on cancer care.
Committed to reducing social and economic inequality. 
Legacy:
He is remembered for his leadership, his contribution to globalization, and his philanthropic endeavors. 
His focus on innovation and his commitment to the Tata Group's values have left a lasting impact. 
Tata is considered one of India's most respected and influential business leaders.
